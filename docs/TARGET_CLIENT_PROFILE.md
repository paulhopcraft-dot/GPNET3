# Preventli — Target Client Profile (ICP)

**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 5 March 2026

---

## Primary Target: SME Employers (50–200 employees)

### Profile
- **Size:** 50–200 employees
- **Sectors:** Construction / trades, Healthcare / aged care
- **Location:** Victoria (WorkSafe jurisdiction, v1)
- **WorkCover premium:** $50K–$300K/year
- **Claim frequency:** 3–15 active claims at any time
- **Current setup:** No dedicated injury manager. HR Manager or office manager handles claims reactively, often using email + spreadsheets

### Pain points
- No visibility across all active claims in one place
- Miss compliance deadlines (certificates, 28-day RTW obligations) — premium increases
- RTW plans created ad hoc, not tracked or documented
- Termination decisions made without audit trail — legal exposure
- Don't know which claims are at risk until it's too late
- WorkSafe audits catch them underprepared

### What Preventli replaces
- Excel/spreadsheet claim tracking
- Manual calendar reminders for certificate expiry
- Email threads with treating practitioners
- Manual RTW plan documents (Word/PDF)

### Value proposition for this segment
> "Your own injury management system without hiring an injury manager. Know exactly what's due, what's at risk, and what to do next — before WorkSafe asks."

### Primary buyer
- **HR Manager / People & Culture Lead** — owns WorkSafe compliance, injury management, RTW obligations
- **Operations Manager** — cares about workers returning productive, covering shifts

### Secondary influencer
- **CFO / Finance** — approves spend; motivated by premium reduction, not claim costs blowing out

## Secondary Target: Mid-Market Employers (200–1,000 employees)

### Profile
- **Size:** 200–1,000 employees
- **Sectors:** Construction / trades, Healthcare / aged care, also manufacturing
- **WorkCover premium:** $300K–$2M+/year
- **Claim frequency:** 15–60+ active claims at any time
- **Current setup:** Dedicated HR team, possibly 1 injury manager. May use a basic case management tool or insurer portal. Compliance is tracked but still manual.

### Pain points
- Injury manager spends 70% of time on admin, not case management
- Hard to see risk patterns across the portfolio (which cases are trending bad)
- RTW plans exist but outcomes aren't tracked — no data to show improvement
- Insurers and brokers asking for reporting they can't produce
- Multiple locations, multiple managers — no consistent process

### What Preventli replaces / augments
- Basic spreadsheet or shared drive case tracking
- Insurer portal (limited, employer-read-only)
- Manual monthly reporting to leadership

### Value proposition for this segment
> "Portfolio-level visibility and compliance automation across all your claims. Give your injury manager their time back — and give leadership the data to reduce your premium."

### Primary buyer
- **HR Manager / People & Culture** — day-to-day champion
- **Risk Manager / Safety Officer** — co-champion; cares about prevention + outcomes data

### Secondary influencer
- **CFO** — premium reduction ROI; if Preventli reduces claim duration by even 10%, the savings dwarf the subscription cost
- **Operations Manager** — productivity impact

## Sector Detail

### Construction / Trades
- **Injury types:** Musculoskeletal (back, shoulder, knee), lacerations, fractures
- **RTW complexity:** Modified duties hard to accommodate on-site — often becomes dispute
- **Compliance risk:** High — subcontractors, PCBU obligations, multi-employer sites
- **Peak pain:** Termination decisions on long-duration claims, managing dispute escalation
- **Hook:** "Know when a claim is heading to conciliation before your insurer tells you"

### Healthcare / Aged Care
- **Injury types:** Manual handling (patient lifts), psychological/stress claims
- **RTW complexity:** Infection control, patient safety limits modified duties options
- **Compliance risk:** High claim frequency, mental health claims increasing fast
- **Peak pain:** Psychological claims — longer duration, harder to manage, more disputes
- **Hook:** "Track mental health claims with the same rigour as physical — before they escalate"

## Qualification Criteria (for beta outreach)

A good beta client has ALL of the following:
- ✅ Based in Victoria (WorkSafe jurisdiction)
- ✅ 50–1,000 employees
- ✅ Construction/trades or healthcare/aged care sector
- ✅ At least 3 active WorkCover claims right now
- ✅ HR Manager or equivalent who owns injury management
- ✅ Frustrated with current process (spreadsheets, missing deadlines, premium increasing)

Bonus:
- Has had a WorkSafe audit or compliance notice in last 2 years
- Premium has increased 2 years running
- Has had a claim go to conciliation or legal proceedings

## What NOT to target in v1

- **Insurers / TAC / Comcare** — different jurisdiction, different data model, post-v2
- **Enterprise (1,000+ employees)** — long sales cycle, procurement, need dedicated CSM
- **Non-Victoria employers** — compliance rules are jurisdiction-specific, v1 is VIC only
- **Industries without physical injury risk** — professional services, retail (low claim frequency, low urgency)
